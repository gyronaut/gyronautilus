There is a certain kind of beauty in the crisis of the universe. The entire facade of meaning and depth contains a self-negating notion that is present no where else. For there is no meaning, no higher purpose, no unknown. And yet, here we are, a collection of molecules that are able to examine ourselves. There is no possibility that the universe has encoded within it any conception of mortality, of right and wrong, good and evil. Existence, by it's very nature, is indifferent in a way unlike anything else: not indifferent due to an amoral lack of empathy to the plights of it's inhabitants, but indifferent due to the fact that there are no differences. There are four ways that particles can interact with each other, each with a corresponding force and "charge", but that's all the differences that exist. Everything else is emergent, illusory. To say that there is some preferred order to the universe can only mean that the universe favors one type of reaction over others. And indeed, that seems to be the case. The only conception of good and evil that the universe has made seems to be that electromagnetism is well and good, but gravity is terrible.
